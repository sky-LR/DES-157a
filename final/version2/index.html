An Inventory Of My Instruments and Their Sounds

Sky LaRoche - DES 157a - Winter 2023 - Every Picture Project

The Steinberger

Headless Horseman

2014

This is my Steinbereger headless bass. It was given to me by my great aunt when I was about 13, which was far to young for me to actually appreciate it. As I’ve gotten older I realized that it was a symbol of my family's support for me pursuing music, which is something I’m eternally grateful for. It went from a weird instrument I never played to my favorite in my collection. I hope to give it to my great nephew someday.

The Toy Piano

Big Kid's Playtime

2022

This is a children’s toy piano I picked up last year at a goodwill for $1.50. I brought it home and immediately launched into the weirdest, funniest jam session I’ve ever been a part of with my roommates. It’s a dinky little machine but it’s now a staple in my collection. Music doesn’t have to be so serious, sometimes the best stuff happens when you just play.

The Les Paul

Daily Driver

2020

This is my Epihpone Les Paul guitar. It feels incredible to play. That’s really what I have to say about it. Playing this guitar is like taking a bite of a nice crunchy grape, or popping bubble wrap, or feeling a piece of sea glass between your fingers. I love having this instrument around because I want to play it. It keeps me playing music and that makes my life better.

The Monotron

Squeaky Little Guy

2016

This is my Korg Monotron pocket synthesizer which I bought when I was 14. It’s possibly the smallest, cheapest synth you could possibly get, but to me at that age it seemed like a massive purchase. For several months I eyed this synth every time I would go to the music store for my bass lesson until I finally saved enough to buy it. Every time I hold the little thing in my hand and hear its squeaky, pre-pubescent sounds I’m reminded of how far I’ve come.

Recording King

Strum Away

2019

This is my one and only acoustic instrument. It’s a Recording King guitar which was gifted to me by my dad and his landlord, Larry. As a music producer, almost all the time I spend making music is on the computer. Recording and editing music takes up massive amounts of time so it’s nice to have portable, non-electronic music I can bring outside, play with friends, or just use to get off my computer now and again.
